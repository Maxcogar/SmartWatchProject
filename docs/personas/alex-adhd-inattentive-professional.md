## ADHD Presentation & Clinical Profile

### Core ADHD-I Symptoms (DSM-5 Based)
- **Attention Difficulties:** Struggles to maintain focus on tasks, especially during meetings or detailed report writing
- **Distractibility:** External stimuli (office chatter, notifications) and internal thoughts frequently derail focus
- **Executive Function Challenges:** Difficulty with task prioritization, time estimation, and project completion
- **Working Memory Issues:** Forgets instructions, loses track of multi-step processes
- **Time Blindness:** Consistently underestimates task duration, struggles with deadlines

### Strengths (Neurological Advantages)
- **Hyperfocus Capability:** Can achieve exceptional productivity on engaging projects for 3-6 hour periods
- **Creative Problem Solving:** Thinks outside conventional patterns, generates innovative marketing campaigns
- **Crisis Performance:** Performs exceptionally well under pressure, thrives during product launches
- **Pattern Recognition:** Excellent at spotting market trends and connecting seemingly unrelated concepts
- **Adaptability:** Quick to pivot strategies based on new information or changing priorities

### Daily Executive Function Profile
- **Peak Focus Hours:** 10 AM - 12 PM, 2 PM - 4 PM (variable daily)
- **Energy Crashes:** Post-lunch (1-2 PM), late afternoon (4-6 PM)
- **Task Switching Cost:** High - needs 5-10 minutes to refocus after interruption
- **Working Memory Span:** 3-4 items max before external capture needed

## ADHD-Specific Challenges & Design Considerations

### Sensory Processing Needs
- **Visual:** High contrast interfaces, minimal visual clutter, consistent typography
- **Auditory:** Customizable notification sounds, option for vibration-only alerts
- **Tactile:** Responsive haptic feedback for confirmation of actions, distinct vibration patterns

### Executive Function Support
- **Working Memory:** Persistent visual reminders of current task and time remaining
- **Time Awareness:** Clear progress indicators and time-left displays
- **Task Switching:** Gentle transitions between modes, clear visual state indicators

### Emotional Regulation Considerations
- **Overwhelm Prevention:** Simple interfaces, limited options, clear primary actions
- **Validation Needs:** Positive reinforcement for completed focus sessions
- **Anxiety Management:** Predictable behavior, reliable notification blocking

### Common ADHD Technology Pitfalls to Avoid
- **Feature Overload:** Too many options leading to analysis paralysis
- **Notification Intrusion:** Breaking focus with non-essential information
- **Complex Navigation:** Multi-step processes that consume working memory
- **Inconsistent Behavior:** Unreliable syncing or unexpected mode changes
- **Abandonment Risk:** Over-complicated setup leading to device abandonment
