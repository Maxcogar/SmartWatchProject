## ADHD Presentation & Clinical Profile

### Core ADHD-H Symptoms (DSM-5 Based)
- **Hyperactivity:** Constantly fidgets with objects, taps feet, shifts position frequently
- **Impulsivity:** Interrupts conversations, makes quick decisions without considering consequences
- **Restlessness:** Feels internal motor that needs constant stimulation, difficulty sitting still during lectures
- **Impatience:** Struggles with waiting in lines, delayed gratification, boring or repetitive tasks
- **Risk-Taking Tendencies:** Engages in stimulating activities, sometimes without considering safety

### Strengths (Neurological Advantages)
- **High Energy:** Can maintain enthusiasm and activity levels others find exhausting
- **Quick Thinking:** Rapid idea generation, excellent at brainstorming sessions
- **Enthusiastic Leadership:** Natural ability to energize groups and drive collaborative projects
- **Crisis Management:** Thrives in high-pressure situations, excellent at handling emergencies
- **Innovation Drive:** Constant need for novelty leads to creative problem-solving approaches
- **Multi-Tasking Ability:** Can juggle multiple activities simultaneously when stimulation is adequate

### Daily Activity & Stimulation Profile
- **Peak Activity Hours:** 8 AM - 11 AM, 7 PM - 11 PM (high energy periods)
- **Stimulation Needs:** Requires background music, fidget tools, or movement during passive activities
- **Attention Regulation:** Needs to alternate between high-stimulation and low-stimulation activities
- **Social Energy:** Draws energy from interaction with others, struggles with isolated study

## ADHD-Specific Challenges & Design Considerations

### Hyperactivity Accommodation
- **Physical Design:** Robust construction to handle fidgeting, spinning, and manipulation
- **Interface Design:** Large touch targets that work with active hands and quick movements
- **Tactile Feedback:** Strong haptic responses that can be felt during physical activity

### Impulsivity Management
- **Quick Actions:** Immediate response to touches and gestures to prevent abandonment
- **Impulse Buffers:** Brief confirmation delays for destructive actions (like ending focus sessions early)
- **Instant Gratification:** Immediate positive feedback for any productive behavior

### Stimulation Regulation
- **Customizable Intensity:** Adjustable vibration strength and visual contrast for different energy levels
- **Background Integration:** Works seamlessly with music, podcasts, or video consumption
- **Movement Compatibility:** Functions remain accessible during walking, cycling, or fidgeting

### Social Connection Needs
- **Achievement Sharing:** Easy ways to share focus session completions with friends
- **Study Buddy Integration:** Connect with classmates for collaborative focus sessions
- **Community Features:** Access to study groups or ADHD peer support networks

## Risk Factors & Mitigation Strategies

### High-Risk Scenarios
- **Device Abandonment:** If interface is too slow or boring, Sam will stop using within days
- **Hyperfocus Transfer:** Focus sessions could become new hyperfocus trap if too engaging
- **Social Comparison:** Achievement tracking could create unhealthy competition or shame

### Mitigation Approaches
- **Rapid Response Design:** All interactions must feel immediate and engaging
- **Built-in Breaks:** Automatic session limits with encouraging messages
- **Privacy Controls:** Option to keep achievements private or share selectively
- **Variety Integration:** Multiple interface themes and interaction patterns to prevent boredom
